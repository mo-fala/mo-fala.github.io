Biblical Greek

The New Testament was (mainly) written in Greek. This is not the modern Greek language of this day and age, but the ancient Greek of many centuries ago. With the drastic changes made to Greek (from what I've heard initiated by the Catholic church), the language has lost a lot of its depth and meaning. Therefore I attempt to analyze ancient Greek as much as I can, rather than analyzing modern Greek.

The great thing about Greek is its richness and depth. Even one word can have so much meaning, as well as so many different meanings. For this reason, the New Testament offers us incredibly much insight. The other side of the coin is that the Greek texts can be interpreted in many different ways. Which interpretation is the correct one? Or was it God's intention to weave many different interpretations into the texts? I think these are very important questions to keep in mind when reading the Bible.
