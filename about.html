About the Original Bible project

The Original Bible project was inspired by a sincere desire to deepen my understanding of God's Word. Although we can learn a lot from translated versions of the Bible, I personally believe a translation does not always lead to the right interpretations. No matter how well you translate, you still lose some of the original meaning of the original text. Next to loss or misinterpretation of the literal meaning, a lot of cultural context is lost as well when we no longer have the original language in front of us. In short: this project seeks to go return to the roots of the Bible, in language and cultural and historical context.

I realize I have taken on quite a task and I am aware of my personal shortcomings in analyzing texts in (ancient) Hebrew and Greek. I was not schooled in these lanuages. The little Hebrew I knew prior to starting this project, was obtained through traveling in Israel, listening to my mom and sister learn Hebrew, and some personal attempts of learning the language. As for Greek, I know my mother-language, Dutch, was influenced significantly by it, but sadly that's how far my knowledge goes. To be frank, I am more equiped to study the Bible in traditional Chinese - which is not meant metaphorically; but based on my actual studies of my Bible in traditional Chinese.
